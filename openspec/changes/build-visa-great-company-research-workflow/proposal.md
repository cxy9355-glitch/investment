## Why

The long-hold research line needs to shift from "which institutions bought" to "why the company itself is great, how that greatness formed, and how institutional behavior can help us learn operation discipline." Visa is a clean first case for turning this company-first research logic into a reusable workflow before expanding to other companies.

## What Changes

- Create a Visa-only great-company research sample under `机构持仓研究`, using a seven-part structure:
  - why the company is great
  - how it became great
  - what proves its greatness
  - how to identify similar greatness
  - how institutions bought, held, sold, or made mistakes
  - what we should learn or challenge
  - current recommendation and archive category based on current valuation
- Create a maintainable core-observation archive table under `机构持仓研究` to record Visa's current archive category and future re-evaluation triggers.
- After the Visa sample is completed and reviewed, create an initial global skill for repeating this workflow on later companies.
- Do not calibrate the template with MCO, AAPL, GOOG, or other companies in this change. Later companies will be used to iterate the skill after the Visa sample is accepted.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `great-company-case-research`: Defines the required structure and quality bar for a single company-first long-hold research case, starting with Visa.
- `core-observation-archive`: Defines the archive categories, table fields, and maintenance rules for tracking whether a researched company should become a core observation sample.

None.
