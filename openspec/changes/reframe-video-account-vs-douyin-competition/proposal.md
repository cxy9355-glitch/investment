## Why

The current research archive has strong raw material, but the thesis is still too loose: it mixes product observation, company strategy, and industry narrative without a single operating question. We need a better exploration proposal that turns "Video Accounts vs Douyin" into a first-principles research program with clear hypotheses, evidence standards, and decision questions.

## What Changes

- Reframe the topic from a narrow product comparison into a competition between two traffic-distribution systems: social-graph-enhanced distribution inside WeChat versus algorithm-first distribution inside Douyin.
- Define a first-principles thesis with one primary question, a fixed set of sub-questions, and explicit falsifiable hypotheses.
- Upgrade the source archive from a reading folder into a traceable evidence system with categories, reading order, and source-to-question mapping.
- Define a competition map that compares Video Accounts and Douyin across strategy, distribution, creator supply, monetization, ecosystem integration, and AI/search extension.
- Produce implementation-ready artifacts for the next phase: thesis memo, evidence tracker, and competition synthesis.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `research-thesis`: Define the primary research question, supporting sub-questions, and falsifiable hypotheses for the Video Accounts vs Douyin thesis.
- `source-archive`: Define how sources are selected, archived, categorized, and tied back to research questions.
- `competition-map`: Define the side-by-side comparison framework and synthesis outputs for strategy, product, ecosystem, and monetization.

### Modified Capabilities
- None.

## Impact

- Affects the research workflow under `video-account-vs-douyin-research/` by turning the archive into a structured evidence base.
- Adds a reusable OpenSpec change package for future iteration, implementation, and discussion.
- Sets the contract for the next deliverables: source annotations, thesis memo, and competition map.
