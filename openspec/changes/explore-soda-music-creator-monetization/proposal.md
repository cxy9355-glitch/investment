## Why

The current discussion on Soda Music creator economics is noisy: growth metrics are visible, but creator income is opaque. We need an exploration package that starts from first principles, separates platform promises from verified cash outcomes, and answers one practical question: whether a creator can build meaningful income on Soda Music, and through which mechanisms.

## What Changes

- Define a research thesis focused on Soda Music creator monetization rather than on app growth alone.
- Build a traceable source archive that distinguishes official platform rules, media reporting, creator anecdotes, and industry benchmarks.
- Produce a conclusion report that separates direct platform income, promotional support, and downstream monetization spillover.
- Capture open questions and evidence gaps so future research can update the thesis rather than restart from zero.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `research-thesis`: Define the primary question, sub-questions, and falsifiable hypotheses for Soda Music creator monetization.
- `source-archive`: Preserve a readable local archive of the selected materials with source quality labels and question mapping.
- `conclusion-report`: Summarize verified monetization paths, income evidence, limitations, and the current best judgment.

### Modified Capabilities
- None.

## Impact

- Adds a reusable OpenSpec exploration package under `openspec/changes/explore-soda-music-creator-monetization/`.
- Establishes a local research archive under `soda-music-creator-research/` for source notes and synthesis.
- Creates a durable base for later comparison against TME, NetEase Cloud Music, or other creator platforms.
