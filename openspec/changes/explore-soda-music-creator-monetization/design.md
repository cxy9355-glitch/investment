## Context

Soda Music has become large enough to matter, but public understanding of its creator economics is still weak. Most public discussion overweights MAU, Douyin traffic, and recommendation experience, while underweighting the actual creator funnel: how musicians enter the platform, what revenue paths are explicit, which payouts are transparent, and how much of the economic upside comes from direct platform settlement versus spillover into promotion, licensing, and performance opportunities.

The goal of this change is not to prove that Soda Music is good or bad for creators. The goal is to build a disciplined exploration frame that can support repeated updating as new evidence appears.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- Start from a single practical question: what does a creator actually earn from Soda Music, directly and indirectly?
- Separate direct cash income from promotional support and downstream opportunities.
- Label evidence quality clearly so official rules, media synthesis, and anecdotal creator feedback are not treated as equivalent.
- Produce a conclusion that is decision-useful even under incomplete disclosure.

**Non-Goals:**
- Estimate an exact universal payout rate per play.
- Build a financial model for ByteDance or a valuation case.
- Exhaustively archive every article about Soda Music.

## Decisions

### 1. Organize the study around creator cash-flow layers

The research will distinguish three layers:

- direct platform income: playback/download/usage-linked settlement or explicit cash incentives
- platform support with monetary implications: promotion funds, traffic support, activity rewards
- downstream spillover income: performance, licensing, label signing, brand and content opportunities created by song exposure

Alternative considered:
- Treat all creator value as one bucket. Rejected because it hides the difference between cash settlement and marketing leverage.

### 2. Use evidence grades

Sources will be labeled:

- `A`: official platform or company material
- `B`: reputable media or industry reporting with concrete details
- `C`: anecdotal creator feedback or secondary summaries

Alternative considered:
- Equal-weight all sources. Rejected because income claims are especially prone to distortion.

### 3. Treat opacity as a finding, not a failure

If Soda Music does not publish a standardized public payout table, that is itself a core conclusion about the platform's creator economics.

Alternative considered:
- Force a pseudo-precise payout estimate from weak data. Rejected because it would overfit anecdotes.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- [Official pages disclose rules but not formulas] -> Mitigation: state clearly what is known, unknown, and contract-dependent.
- [Success-case bias in media reporting] -> Mitigation: distinguish average creator economics from hit-song economics.
- [Traffic spillover is hard to monetize directly] -> Mitigation: keep direct income and downstream opportunity as separate layers.

## Migration Plan

1. Define the thesis and sub-questions.
2. Archive the highest-signal sources as readable local notes.
3. Write a source index with quality labels and question mapping.
4. Produce a conclusion memo with confidence, caveats, and next-step questions.

## Open Questions

- How large is the gap between a median creator's direct Soda Music income and a breakout creator's total spillover income?
- Does Soda Music eventually move toward more transparent standardized payout communication?
- How much creator value depends on Douyin-side usage rather than on standalone Soda Music listening?
